Abstract

This article walks through the history of pagers, from becoming one of the essential communication tools in healthcare, emergency services, and the corporate world in the 1980s to the 1990s. The article discusses how the use of pagers raised privacy issues and ethical concerns for example signal intercepts and that they can be exploited by electronic eavesdropping. The article then investigates how pagers can be used as a military and political tool, supported by an analysis of the ways in which pagers were weaponized during the 2024 Israel-Hezbollah conflict to highlight the risks associated with weaponizing communication technology. The conclusion highlights the conflicting nature of technological innovations and suggests that further investigation be conducted to prevent any unlikely negative consequences.

Introduction

Long before the smartphone development and the world of instant messaging, pagers were considered to be some of the most high-end communications tech there was. These small, portable devices revolutionized the way people stayed connected, particularly in critical industries such as healthcare, emergency services, and corporate sectors (Smith, 2022). However, the rise and fall of pagers also unveil the often-overlooked darker aspects of engineering advancements.

The Rise of Pagers

Pagers, which are often referred to as beepers, emerged in the 80s-90s. They enabled a simple, yet efficient means of sending and receiving short messages that kept users informed of important things happening no matter where they were. The tech was simple: a receiver in the pager receives an alert signal from a transmitter and then sounds an alert (whether with a beep or by vibrating), to notify the user, who can then read a short message. Pagers were first adopted in healthcare environments, allowing doctors to be reached quickly in emergencies. The quick and reliable communication they facilitated was a game-changer, saving countless lives (Smith, 2022). Also for those emergency personnel and business people to always be reachable, the pager was a necessity as well.

The Shadow Side - Privacy Concerns

Just like all leaps in technology, pagers had their drawbacks, the most significant being privacy concerns. Since pagers transmitted signals over public airwaves, they were naturally open to interception. Eavesdropping on pager messages became a relatively simple task for those with the right equipment, leading to significant concerns about the confidentiality of sensitive information (Brown, 2021). This vulnerability was particularly troubling in the medical field, where the privacy of patient information is crucial. Engineers and developers faced ethical dilemmas in balancing innovation with the responsibility to protect users' data (Freundlich, Freundlich & Drolet, 2018).

Engineering for Control and Surveillance

The widespread use of pagers also highlighted a darker aspect of engineering: the potential for control and surveillance. Employers and organizations could keep tabs on their employees' whereabouts and availability at all times, blurring the lines between professional and personal life. This constant connectivity, while beneficial for productivity, raised questions about autonomy and work-life balance (Fuggle, Harrison & MacArthur, 2021). Unfortunately, those did get misused from time to time when it came to tracking people down via their pager, raising some ethical questions surrounding wiretapping. The same technology that was supposed to facilitate connection and productivity also took with it an added level of monitoring and, at times, controlling individuals.

Pagers in Political and Military Contexts

Pagers functioned equivalent to mobile phones for communication and also had some important roles in the political as well as military fields. For instance, pagers were used in the Israel-Hezbollah conflict. In September 2024, a series of explosions involving pagers and walkie-talkies occurred across Lebanon, resulting in significant casualties and injuries. These devices, which were used by Hezbollah members, were fitted with explosives and detonated remotely, causing widespread panic and chaos (Jones, 2024).This manner of using the pagers emphasises the dark side of new advanced technologies. The weaponising of everyday communication devices is a threat that stretches beyond immediate bodily harm and to the wider context of psychological warfare. The explosions in Lebanon demonstrated how easily accessible technology could be repurposed for malicious intent, raising concerns about the security of other widely used technologies (Jones, 2024). Potential Risks with Modern Technologies

More broadly, the incident in Lebanon offers an alarming example of what could go wrong with modern technologies dependent on software and radio connectivity â€” from smartphones to electric cars. In the event of a conflict, these technologies could be similarly exploited, leading to unprecedented consequences. The integration of software in these devices makes them vulnerable to hacking and remote manipulation, which could be used to disrupt communication, transportation, and even critical infrastructure (Doe, 2023).The reasonable fright and concern following the Lebanon attacks underline the need for robust security measures and ethical considerations in the growth and deployment of technology. As we continue to innovate and integrate technology into our daily lives, it is crucial to remain attentive to the potential for misuse and to implement safeguards that protect against such threats (Doe, 2023).

Conclusion

The example of pagers is a testament to the double-edged sword of engineering advancements. Although they helped in bridging communication barriers, particularly in sectors as essential as healthcare and finance, they also brought to light sizeable ethical and privacy concerns. Using technology for nefarious purposes, as we have seen with Lebanon this year is a dangerous path that we should embark on. The history of pagers has evolved from a ground-breaking innovation to an outdated relic of the past, but it is a lesson we can use moving forward in tech â€” how will we balance producing new technology when taking into account the consequences of such advances?
